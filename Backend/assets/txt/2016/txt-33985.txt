Artikel 8. - Niet volstorte aande-len.
  Elke inschrijver op aandelen is verbonden voor het totale bedrag van zijn aandelen,
niettegen-staande elke hiermee strijdige bepaling.
Behoudens in geval van geldige vermindering van het kapitaal kan hij niet ontheven worden van zijn
stortings-plicht.
De verbin-tenis tot volstor-ting van een aandeel is onvoor-waardelijk en ondeel-baar.
Indien niet volstorte aandelen aan verscheidene personen in onver-deeld-heid toebehoren, staat
ieder van hen in voor de betaling van het gehele bedrag van de opgevraagde opeisbare stortingen.
Bijstorting of volstor-ting wordt door de algemene vergadering opge-vraagd op een door haar te
bepalen tijdstip. Aan de vennoot wordt hiervan bij aangete-kende brief kennis gegeven met opgave
van een girore-kening waarop de betaling bij uitsluiting van elke andere wijze van betaling door
overschrijving of storting dient te worden gedaan. De vennoot komt in verzuim door het enkel
ver-strijken van de in de kennis-geving bepaalde termijn en er is rente verschuldigd tegen de op dat
tijdstip vastgestelde wettelijke rentevoet vermeerderd met twee ten honderd.
Zolang de opgevraagde opeisbare stortingen op een aandeel niet zijn gedaan volgens deze
bepaling, blijft de uitoefening van de daaraan verbonden rechten geschorst.
Artikel 9. - Overdracht.
De aandelen van een vennoot mogen op straf van nietigheid noch afgestaan worden onder
levenden, noch overge-dragen worden ingevolge overlijden, dan met de instemming van ten minste
de helft der vennoten, die ten minste de drie/vierden van het kapitaal bezitten, na aftrek van de
rechten waarvan de overdracht is voorgesteld.
Deze instemming is niet vereist wanneer de afstand geschiedt in voordeel van een medeven-noot, de
echtgenoot van de overdrager of van de erflater, of van zijn afstammelin-gen in rechte lijn.
De vennoot die één of meer aandelen wil overdragen, moet van zijn voornemen aan de vennoten
kennis geven bij een aangetekende brief, waarin hij de naam, de voor-naam, het beroep en de
woonplaats van de voorgestelde overnemer meedeelt, alsook het aantal aandelen dat hij wenst over
te dragen, en de voorwaarden van de over-dracht.                     
Indien de vennoten in gebreke blijven binnen een maand na ontvangst te antwoorden op het verzoek
tot goedkeu-ring van de overdracht, hun bij aangetekende brief toegezonden, zullen zij geacht
worden zich niet te verzetten.
Tegen weigering van goedkeuring, zelfs van één vennoot, staat geen beroep op de rechter open.
Nochtans, indien de vennoot die zijn aandelen wenst over te dragen, het verlangt, zijn de weigerende
vennoten ver-plicht binnen drie maan-den, hetzij de aandelen zelf te kopen, hetzij er een koper voor
te vinden. Indien de weigerende vennoten de aandelen zelf kopen, vindt het recht van voorkeur
overeen-komstige toepas-sing.       
In dit geval zal de verkoopprijs gelijk zijn aan de werkelijke waarde op de dag van de overdracht. Bij
gebreke aan overeenstemming tussen partijen zal de werkelijke waarde worden vastgesteld door
twee deskundi-gen waarvan één door elke partij te benoe-men binnen de twintig dagen nadat zij
hiertoe werden verzocht bij aangetekende brief. Over menings-verschillen tussen de twee
deskundigen zal worden beslist door een derde deskundige, die, op hun verzoek of op verzoek van
één der partijen, zal worden aangewezen door de voorzit-ter van de Rechtbank van Koophandel van
de plaats waar de vennootschap gevestigd is. Tegen de beslissing van de derde deskundige staat
geen rechtsmiddel open.
De afkoop van de aandelen zal in elk geval tot stand moeten komen binnen drie maanden na de dag
waarop de waarde op de hierboven bepaalde wijze defini-tief zal zijn vastge-steld. Zoniet kan de
overdrager de ontbinding van de ven-nootschap vorderen, op voorwaarde dat hij deze eis instelt
binnen de veertig dagen na het verstrij-ken van deze termijn.                    
De overname-prijs, in gemeen akkoord, of na expertise, zal moeten betaald zijn binnen de drie
maanden na het ver-strijken van de termijn bedongen in de aanmaning door de overdrager tot
verplichte overname door de weigerende venno-ten.

respectieve-lijke aandelen.

Article 10. - Shares in Indivisibility.
  If a share belongs to several different owners, the exercise of the rights attaching to them shall be
suspended until one single person is appointed in writing as the owner of the share in respect of the
company.
If a share is encumbered with usufruct, unless the usufructuary and the bare owner appoint either of
themselves in writing, the rights attaching to the share are to be exercised by the usufructuary.
 
 
CHAPTER III. - MANAGEMENT AND SUPERVISION.
Article 11. – Composition of the Board of Business managers. The company is to be managed by
one or more business managers, whether partners or not, appointed by the General Meeting of the
Partners.
Article 12. - Powers of the business managers. Each business manager, acting alone, may perform
all acts which are necessary or useful for the achievement of the company aim, except those acts for
which according to the Companies Code the General Meeting is solely competent.
Each business manager represents the company in respect of third parties and in court as plaintiff or
defendant.
The business manager or the business managers acting together, are authorised either to transfer
the day-to-day management, or to transfer the management of company business to a director who
may be a fellow partner or not, or only to transfer specific special powers to an agent.

Article 13. - Supervision.
Reserving the stipulations in the event of the company numbering only one partner, the following
rules apply.
The audit of the financial situation, the annual statement of accounts and the lawfulness of the
transactions, to be reflected in the annual statement of accounts, is to be entrusted to one or more
Supervisory Board Members. The Supervisory Board Members are to be appointed by the General
Meeting of Shareholders, from amongst the members of the Institute of Company Auditors. The
Supervisory Board Members are to be appointed for a renewable period of three years.
 
As long as the company satisfies the provisions of Article 141 of the Companies Code, no
Supervisory Board Member shall be appointed; and each partner shall have the powers of inspection
and audit of a Supervisory Board Member, and may, at his own cost, call upon the assistance of an
accountant for this purpose.
Even if the company satisfies the provisions of Article 141 of the Companies Code, the General
Meeting still retains the right to appoint a Supervisory Board Member from the members of the
Institute of Company Auditors, by simple majority of votes.
 
CHAPTER IV. - GENERAL MEETING.
Article 14. - General meeting. An Ordinary General Meeting shall be held each year, at the actual
registered office or at any other place mentioned in the convening notice, on the 2nd Tuesday of the
month of June at 8 p.m.
If this day is a statutory holiday, the meeting shall be held on the very next working day.
  However, the meeting may be convened by the business manager whenever the interests of the
company dictate this.
The business managers and, if any exist, the Supervisory Board Members, may convene the General
Meeting. They must convene it whenever partners representing one-fifth of the registered capital
request this.
The notices convening the General Meeting are to mention the agenda including the subjects for
discussion.
They are to be sent to the partners, holders of certificates issued with the cooperation of the
company, holders of bonds, Supervisory Board Members and business managers, by registered
letter fifteen days before the meeting.
Together with the letter convening the General Meeting, the partners, Supervisory Board Members
and business managers are to be sent a copy of the documents required to be made available to
them pursuant to the Companies Code.
A copy of these documents is to be sent straight away and free of charge to the other persons called
to attend who request this.
A list of attendants is to be drawn up at each General Meeting.
Each share gives a right to one vote.
The minutes of the General Meetings are to be signed by the members of the bureau, and by those
partners who request this. Copies for third parties are to be signed by one business manager.
 
 
CHAPTER V. - COMPANY DOCUMENTS – DISTRIBUTION OF PROFITS.
Article 15. – Financial year of the company. The financial year of the company commences on the
first of January and ends on the thirty-first of December of each year.
The stipulations relating to inventories and annual statements of account correspond to the rules
provided in Article 92 of the Companies Code.
  Article 16. - Distribution of Profits. The credit balance of the annual statement of accounts, after
deduction of charges, overheads, the necessary
 
depreciation and fiscal provisions, is the net profit of the company and shall be distributed in line with
the decision of the General Meeting, which may decide to pay part of the distributable profit to the
partners as dividends. The business manager is authorised to determine the time at which dividends
are paid.
Each year, at least five percent of this profit is to be deducted in advance to form a reserve fund. This
advance deduction ceases to be required when the legal reserve has reached one-tenth of the
registered capital.

CHAPTER VIII. – PROVISIONS APPLICABLE IN THE EVENT OF THE

ONLY ONE PARTNER.
Article 20. - General Provision.
  All of the provisions of the present Articles of Association are applicable when the company
numbers only one partner, provided they do not conflict with the rules relating to management by one
partner stipulated below.
Article 21. - Transfer of shares.
a) The sole partner alone shall decide to transfer all or part of his shares.
b) If the sole partner dies and his shares do not pass to any title holder, the company shall be lawfully
wound up and Article 343 of the Companies Code shall be applied.
c) The death of a sole partner, who leaves heirs or legatees, shall not result in the winding-up of the
company.
Until the day of the division of the shares or until the issue of the bequests relating to these shares,
the rights attaching to these shares are
 
to be exercised by the heirs or legatees lawfully entering into or placed in ownership, in proportion to
their rights to the estate.
The person who inherits the usufruct of the shares of the sole partner shall exercise the rights
attaching to them.
 
 
 
 
  Article 22. - Capital increase - preferential right.
  If the sole partner decides to perform a capital increase in species, Article 7 of these Articles of
Association shall not apply.
  Article 23. - Business manager - Appointment.
If no business manager is appointed, the sole partner shall lawfully have all of the rights and
obligations of a business manager. Both the sole partner and a third party may be appointed
business manager.
Article 24. - Dismissal.
If a third party is appointed business manager, even in the Articles of Association and without time
limit, he may be dismissed at any time by the sole partner, unless he is appointed for a specific
period or for an unspecified period subject to notice.
Article 25. - Supervision.
As long as the company has no Supervisory Board Member and a third party is business manager,
the sole partner shall exercise all of the powers of the Supervisory Board Member, as stipulated in
Article 13 of these Articles of Association.
As long as the sole partner is also the business manager and no Supervisory Board Member has
been appointed, no inspection shall exist within the company.

powers. Minutes are to be drawn up of the sole partner’s decisions, which are to be signed by him
and afterwards recorded in a register that is to be kept at the company’s registered office.
